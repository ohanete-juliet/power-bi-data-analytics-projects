Project Overview
This project analyzes reported shark attacks over the past century to uncover trends, high-risk locations, injury patterns, and shark species involvement.

Objectives
- Analyze trends in shark attacks over time
- Identify countries and locations with the highest number of attacks
- Determine the most commonly injured body parts
- Analyze the most frequently involved shark species

Tools Used
- Power BI
- Power Query
- Excel

Key Insights
- Shark attacks have increased over time, especially after the mid-20th century.
- The USA and Australia report the highest number of attacks.
- Attacks mostly occur in coastal recreational areas.
- Legs and arms are the most frequently injured body parts.
- Certain species such as White Sharks appear more frequently in reported attacks.

Recommendations
- Increase safety awareness in high-risk coastal locations.
- Improve monitoring and public education around peak risk areas.
- Encourage safer ocean practices.
